<p><i><b>Moskva</b></i> (<a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">Russian</a>: <span lang="ru">Москва <small></small><span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Russian" title="Help:IPA/Russian">[mɐskˈva]</a></span></span>, <small><a href="/wiki/Literal_translation" title="Literal translation">lit.</a>&#8201;</small>&#39;Moscow&#39;), formerly <i><b>Slava</b></i> (<a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">Russian</a>: <span lang="ru">Слава</span>, <small><a href="/wiki/Literal_translation" title="Literal translation">lit.</a>&#8201;</small>&#39;Glory&#39;), was a <a href="/wiki/Guided_missile_cruiser" class="mw-redirect" title="Guided missile cruiser">guided missile cruiser</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Russian_Navy" title="Russian Navy">Russian Navy</a>. The ship was the <a href="/wiki/Lead_ship" title="Lead ship">lead ship</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Slava-class_cruiser" title="Slava-class cruiser">Project 1164 <i>Atlant</i> class</a>, named after the city of Moscow. The <a href="/wiki/Flagship" title="Flagship">flagship</a> of the Russian <a href="/wiki/Black_Sea_Fleet" title="Black Sea Fleet">Black Sea Fleet</a>, and with a crew of 510, <i>Moskva</i> was considered the most powerful warship in the Black Sea region.<sup id="cite_ref-BBC_14_April_2022_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBC_14_April_2022-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> 
</p><p>The cruiser was deployed in military conflicts in <a href="/wiki/Russo-Georgian_War" title="Russo-Georgian War">Georgia (2008)</a>, <a href="/wiki/Capture_of_Southern_Naval_Base" title="Capture of Southern Naval Base">Crimea (2014)</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Russian_military_intervention_in_the_Syrian_civil_war" title="Russian military intervention in the Syrian civil war">Syria (2015)</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Beginning in February 2022, it led the naval assault during the <a href="/wiki/2022_Russian_invasion_of_Ukraine" title="2022 Russian invasion of Ukraine">2022 Russian invasion of Ukraine</a>, until <a href="/wiki/Sinking_of_the_Moskva" title="Sinking of the Moskva">its sinking</a> on 14 April 2022.<sup id="cite_ref-Ljunggren_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Ljunggren-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-tass_14_April_2022_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tass_14_April_2022-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Cruiser_Slava_aerial_starboard_1983.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/60/Cruiser_Slava_aerial_starboard_1983.jpg/220px-Cruiser_Slava_aerial_starboard_1983.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="146" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/60/Cruiser_Slava_aerial_starboard_1983.jpg/330px-Cruiser_Slava_aerial_starboard_1983.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/60/Cruiser_Slava_aerial_starboard_1983.jpg/440px-Cruiser_Slava_aerial_starboard_1983.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3000" data-file-height="1986" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Cruiser_Slava_aerial_starboard_1983.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><i>Slava</i> <abbr title="circa">c.</abbr> 1983</div></div></div>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="As_Slava">As <i>Slava</i></span></h3>
<p><i>Slava</i> was <a href="/wiki/Keel_laying" title="Keel laying">laid down</a> in 1976 in Shipyard 445 of the <a href="/wiki/61_Kommunara_Shipbuilding_Plant" class="mw-redirect" title="61 Kommunara Shipbuilding Plant">61 Kommunara Shipbuilding Plant</a> in <a href="/wiki/Mykolaiv" title="Mykolaiv">Mykolaiv</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ukrainian_Soviet_Socialist_Republic" title="Ukrainian Soviet Socialist Republic">Ukrainian SSR</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ship_launching" class="mw-redirect" title="Ship launching">launched</a> in 1979, and <a href="/wiki/Ship_commissioning" title="Ship commissioning">commissioned</a> on 30 January 1983. Between 18 and 22 November 1986, the ship visited the Greek port of <a href="/wiki/Piraeus" title="Piraeus">Piraeus</a>.
</p><p><i>Slava</i> played a role in the <a href="/wiki/Malta_Summit" title="Malta Summit">Malta Summit</a> (2–3 December 1989) between <a href="/wiki/Leader_of_the_Soviet_Union" class="mw-redirect" title="Leader of the Soviet Union">Soviet leader</a> <a href="/wiki/Mikhail_Gorbachev" title="Mikhail Gorbachev">Mikhail Gorbachev</a> and US President <a href="/wiki/George_H._W._Bush" title="George H. W. Bush">George H. W. Bush</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:3_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:3-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> She was used by the Soviet delegation, while the US delegation had their sleeping quarters aboard <a href="/wiki/USS_Belknap_(CG-26)" title="USS Belknap (CG-26)">USS&#160;<i>Belknap</i></a>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> The ships were anchored in a <a href="/wiki/Roadstead" title="Roadstead">roadstead</a> off the coast of <a href="/wiki/Marsaxlokk" title="Marsaxlokk">Marsaxlokk</a>. Stormy weather and choppy seas resulted in some meetings being cancelled or rescheduled, and gave rise to the moniker the "Seasick Summit" among international media. In the end, the meetings took place aboard <a href="/wiki/TS_Maxim_Gorkiy" title="TS Maxim Gorkiy"><i>Maxim Gorkiy</i></a>, a Soviet <a href="/wiki/Cruise_ship" title="Cruise ship">cruise ship</a> anchored in Marsaxlokk Bay.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><i>Slava</i> returned to <a href="/wiki/Mykolaiv" title="Mykolaiv">Mykolaiv</a> in December 1990 for a refit that lasted until late 1998.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> On 16 May 1996 the ship was formally renamed <i>Moskva</i>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2022)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="As_Moskva">As <i>Moskva</i></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Project_1164_Moskva_2009_G1.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c9/Project_1164_Moskva_2009_G1.jpg/220px-Project_1164_Moskva_2009_G1.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="119" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c9/Project_1164_Moskva_2009_G1.jpg/330px-Project_1164_Moskva_2009_G1.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c9/Project_1164_Moskva_2009_G1.jpg/440px-Project_1164_Moskva_2009_G1.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2950" data-file-height="1600" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Project_1164_Moskva_2009_G1.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><i>Moskva</i> in 2009</div></div></div>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Project_1164_Moskva_2012_G2.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5b/Project_1164_Moskva_2012_G2.jpg/220px-Project_1164_Moskva_2012_G2.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="138" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5b/Project_1164_Moskva_2012_G2.jpg/330px-Project_1164_Moskva_2012_G2.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5b/Project_1164_Moskva_2012_G2.jpg/440px-Project_1164_Moskva_2012_G2.jpg 2x" data-file-width="5100" data-file-height="3200" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Project_1164_Moskva_2012_G2.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><i>Moskva</i> in 2012</div></div></div>
<p>Recommissioned in April 2000, <i>Moskva</i> replaced the <a href="/wiki/Kynda-class_cruiser" title="Kynda-class cruiser">Kynda-class</a> cruiser <a href="/wiki/Soviet_cruiser_Admiral_Golovko" title="Soviet cruiser Admiral Golovko"><i>Admiral Golovko</i></a> as the <a href="/wiki/Flagship" title="Flagship">flagship</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Government_of_Russia" title="Government of Russia">Russian</a> <a href="/wiki/Black_Sea_Fleet" title="Black Sea Fleet">Black Sea Fleet</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In early April 2003, <i>Moskva</i>, along with the frigate <a href="/wiki/Soviet_frigate_Pytlivyy" title="Soviet frigate Pytlivyy"><i>Pytlivyy</i></a>, <i>Smetlivy</i>, and a landing ship departed Sevastopol for exercises in the Indian Ocean with a <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Fleet_(Russia)" title="Pacific Fleet (Russia)">Pacific Fleet</a> task group (<i>Marshal Shaposhnikov</i> and <i>Admiral Panteleyev</i>) and the <a href="/wiki/Indian_Navy" title="Indian Navy">Indian Navy</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> The force was supported by the <a href="/wiki/Boris_Chilikin-class_fleet_oiler" title="Boris Chilikin-class fleet oiler">Project 1559V tanker</a> <i>Ivan Bubnov</i> and the Project 712 <a href="/wiki/Tugboat" title="Tugboat">ocean-going tug</a> <i>Shakhter</i>.
</p><p><i>Moskva</i> visited Malta's <a href="/wiki/Grand_Harbour" title="Grand Harbour">Grand Harbour</a> in October 2004, and the Ensemble of the Black Sea Fleet performed at a concert at the <a href="/wiki/Mediterranean_Conference_Centre" title="Mediterranean Conference Centre">Mediterranean Conference Centre</a> in <a href="/wiki/Valletta" title="Valletta">Valletta</a> for the occasion.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> In 2008 and 2009, she visited the Mediterranean and participated in naval drills with the ships of the Northern Fleet.<sup id="cite_ref-Interfax.ru_2015_418490_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Interfax.ru_2015_418490-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In August 2008, in support of the <a href="/wiki/2008_South_Ossetia_war" class="mw-redirect" title="2008 South Ossetia war">Russian invasion of Georgia</a>, <i>Moskva</i> was deployed to secure the <a href="/wiki/Black_Sea" title="Black Sea">Black Sea</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> During a brief surface engagement, the <a href="/wiki/Georgian_Navy" class="mw-redirect" title="Georgian Navy">Georgian Navy</a> scored one missile hit on <i>Moskva</i> before being overwhelmed.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> After Russia's recognition of <a href="/wiki/Abkhazia" title="Abkhazia">Abkhazia</a>'s independence, the ship was stationed at the Abkhazian capital, <a href="/wiki/Sukhumi" title="Sukhumi">Sukhumi</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On 3 December 2009, <i>Moskva</i> was laid up for a month at floating dock PD-30 in Sevastopol for a scheduled interim overhaul which comprised replacement of cooling and other machinery, reclamation work at the bottom and outboard fittings, propulsion shafts and screws, clearing and painting of bottom and above-water parts of the ship's hull.
</p><p>In April 2010 it was reported that the cruiser would join other navy units in the Indian Ocean to conduct exercises.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> In August 2013 the cruiser visited <a href="/wiki/Havana" title="Havana">Havana</a>, Cuba.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In late August 2013, <i>Moskva</i> was deployed to the Mediterranean Sea in response to the build-up of US warships along the coast of Syria.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> During the <a href="/wiki/2014_Crimean_crisis" class="mw-redirect" title="2014 Crimean crisis">Russian invasion of Crimea</a> in 2014, <i>Moskva</i> blockaded the Ukrainian fleet in <a href="/wiki/Donuzlav_Lake" class="mw-redirect" title="Donuzlav Lake">Donuzlav Lake</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On 17 September 2014, <i>Moskva</i> was deployed to the Mediterranean Sea, taking shift from guard ship <i>Pytlivy</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-Interfax.ru_2015_418490_17-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Interfax.ru_2015_418490-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In July 2015, <i>Moskva</i> visited <a href="/wiki/Luanda" title="Luanda">Luanda</a>, to strengthen military cooperation with Angola.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> From the end of September 2015, while in the eastern Mediterranean, the cruiser was charged with the air defences for the Russian aviation group based near the Syrian town of <a href="/wiki/Latakia" title="Latakia">Latakia</a> that conducted the <a href="/wiki/Russian_military_intervention_in_the_Syrian_Civil_War" class="mw-redirect" title="Russian military intervention in the Syrian Civil War">air campaign</a> in Syria.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> On 25 November 2015, after the <a href="/wiki/2015_Russian_Sukhoi_Su-24_shootdown" title="2015 Russian Sukhoi Su-24 shootdown">2015 Russian Sukhoi Su-24 shootdown</a>, it was reported that <i>Moskva</i>, armed with the <a href="/wiki/S-300F/RIF" class="mw-redirect" title="S-300F/RIF">S-300F</a> surface-to-air missile system,<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> would be deployed near the coastal Syria-Turkey border.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> In 2016, she was replaced by <a href="/wiki/Sister_ship" title="Sister ship">sister ship</a> <a href="/wiki/Russian_cruiser_Varyag_(1983)" title="Russian cruiser Varyag (1983)"><i>Varyag</i></a> in the eastern Mediterranean Sea.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> On 22 July 2016 <i>Moskva</i> was awarded the <a href="/wiki/Order_of_Nakhimov" title="Order of Nakhimov">Order of Nakhimov</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Upon return from her deployment in January 2016, <i>Moskva</i> was to undergo a refit and upgrade but due to lack of funds her future remained uncertain as of July 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In June 2019, <i>Moskva</i> left the port of Sevastopol in the Black Sea to test her combat systems and main propulsion.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In February 2020, Russian news agency <a href="/wiki/TASS" title="TASS">TASS</a> reported that a very "rare and important" Christian relic purported to be a part of the <a href="/wiki/True_Cross" title="True Cross">True Cross</a> on which <a href="/wiki/Jesus" title="Jesus">Jesus</a> was <a href="/wiki/Crucifixion" title="Crucifixion">crucified</a> was to be placed aboard the ship.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On 3 July 2020, <i>Moskva</i> completed two and a half months of repairs and maintenance intended to allow her to remain in service until 2040.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> The first post-repair deployment was scheduled for August 2020; however, in reality, she only began to prepare for the deployment in February 2021.<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> She was at sea on exercises in March 2021.<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2022_Russian_invasion_of_Ukraine">2022 Russian invasion of Ukraine</span></h4>
<link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1033289096"/><div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Attack_on_Snake_Island" title="Attack on Snake Island">Attack on Snake Island</a></div><p>Moskva, the <a href="/wiki/Flagship" title="Flagship">flagship</a> of the Russian <a href="/wiki/Black_Sea_Fleet" title="Black Sea Fleet">Black Sea Fleet</a>, helped lead the naval assault during the <a href="/wiki/2022_Russian_invasion_of_Ukraine" title="2022 Russian invasion of Ukraine">2022 Russian invasion of Ukraine</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Ljunggren_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Ljunggren-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-tass_14_April_2022_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tass_14_April_2022-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> She was the most powerful surface vessel in the Black Sea region at the time.<sup id="cite_ref-BBC_14_April_2022_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBC_14_April_2022-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> 
</p><p>In February 2022, the cruiser left <a href="/wiki/Sevastopol_Naval_Base" title="Sevastopol Naval Base">Sevastopol</a> to participate in the attack on Ukraine.<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup> The ship was later used against the Ukrainian armed forces during the <a href="/wiki/Attack_on_Snake_Island" title="Attack on Snake Island">attack on Snake Island</a>, together with the Russian <a href="/wiki/Patrol_boat" title="Patrol boat">patrol boat</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Russian_patrol_boat_Vasily_Bykov" title="Russian patrol boat Vasily Bykov">Vasily Bykov</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup> <i>Moskva</i> hailed the island's garrison over the radio and demanded its surrender, and was told "<a href="/wiki/Russian_warship,_go_fuck_yourself" title="Russian warship, go fuck yourself">Russian warship, go fuck yourself</a>". After this, all contact was lost with Snake Island, and the thirteen-member Ukrainian garrison was captured.<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup> The Slava class cruisers are built for <a href="/wiki/Air_supremacy" title="Air supremacy">air superiority</a> and they have no <a href="/wiki/Land-attack_missile" title="Land-attack missile">land-attack missiles</a>. <i>Moskva</i> mainly stayed behind other Russian warships, providing air cover for <a href="/wiki/Demonstration_(military)" title="Demonstration (military)">military demonstrations</a> of <a href="/wiki/Amphibious_warfare" title="Amphibious warfare">amphibious landings</a> with Odesa as the apparent target.<sup id="cite_ref-nnSutton2_45-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nnSutton2-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sinking">Sinking</span></h2>
<link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1033289096"/><div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Sinking_of_the_Moskva" title="Sinking of the Moskva">Sinking of the <i>Moskva</i></a></div>
<div class="mw-kartographer-container thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width: 300px;"><a class="mw-kartographer-map" style="width: 300px; height: 200px;" data-mw="interface" data-style="osm-intl" data-width="300" data-height="200" data-zoom="6" data-lat="46.264" data-lon="31.955" data-overlays="[&quot;_3632eacd60aee5a6e3c919070cd8b908dd567084&quot;]" href="/wiki/Special:Map/6/46.264/31.955/en"><img src="https://maps.wikimedia.org/img/osm-intl,6,46.264,31.955,300x200.png?lang=en&amp;domain=en.wikipedia.org&amp;title=Russian+cruiser+Moskva&amp;groups=_3632eacd60aee5a6e3c919070cd8b908dd567084" alt="" width="300" height="200" decoding="async" srcset="https://maps.wikimedia.org/img/osm-intl,6,46.264,31.955,300x200@2x.png?lang=en&amp;domain=en.wikipedia.org&amp;title=Russian+cruiser+Moskva&amp;groups=_3632eacd60aee5a6e3c919070cd8b908dd567084 2x" /></a><div class="thumbcaption">Location of <i>Moskva</i> in Black Sea on 12 and 13 April 2022 according to satellite imagery<sup id="cite_ref-BBC_Sunken_46-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBC_Sunken-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-nnSutton3_47-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nnSutton3-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup></div></div></div>
<p><span class="anchor" id="Sinking_of_Moskva"></span>
</p><p>Ukrainian presidential adviser <a href="/wiki/Oleksiy_Arestovych" title="Oleksiy Arestovych">Oleksiy Arestovych</a> and <a href="/wiki/Governor_of_Odessa_Oblast" title="Governor of Odessa Oblast">Odesa governor</a> <a href="/wiki/Maksym_Marchenko" title="Maksym Marchenko">Maksym Marchenko</a> said their forces hit <i>Moskva</i> in the late hours of 13 April 2022, with two <a href="/wiki/R-360_Neptune" title="R-360 Neptune">R-360 Neptune</a> <a href="/wiki/Anti-ship_missile" title="Anti-ship missile">anti-ship missiles</a>, and she was on fire around 7 p.m. local time (GMT+3).<sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-nnSutton3_47-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nnSutton3-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On 14 April 2022, the <a href="/wiki/Operational_Command_South" title="Operational Command South">Ukrainian Southern Command</a> said that <i>Moskva</i> had <a href="/wiki/Capsizing" title="Capsizing">capsized</a> and was beginning to sink.<sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup> Ukrainian officials and the <a href="/wiki/US_Department_of_Defense" class="mw-redirect" title="US Department of Defense">US Department of Defense</a> said Ukraine attacked the cruiser with two <a href="/wiki/R-360_Neptune" title="R-360 Neptune">R-360 Neptune</a> <a href="/wiki/Anti-ship_missile" title="Anti-ship missile">anti-ship missiles</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-pentagon-assessment_50-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pentagon-assessment-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Ministry_of_Defence_(Russia)" title="Ministry of Defence (Russia)">Russian Ministry of Defence</a> said a fire caused a munitions explosion.<sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup> The same day, the Russian Navy attempted to tow the damaged ship toward <a href="/wiki/Sevastopol" title="Sevastopol">Sevastopol</a>, but she sank in the <a href="/wiki/Black_Sea" title="Black Sea">Black Sea</a>, 100 kilometres (62&#160;mi) from the coast of <a href="/wiki/Odessa" title="Odessa">Odessa</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52">&#91;52&#93;</a></sup> <i>Moskva</i> is the largest warship to be sunk in combat since <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>, and the first of a similar size since the <a href="/wiki/ARA_General_Belgrano" title="ARA General Belgrano">ARA <i>General Belgrano</i></a> during the <a href="/wiki/Falklands_War" title="Falklands War">Falklands War</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-53" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-53">&#91;53&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-54" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-54">&#91;54&#93;</a></sup>  
</p><p>According to the Lithuanian defense minister, there were 485 crew members aboard, including 66 officers. He also said that a "Turkish ship" responded to a distress call and saved 54 crew members at 2&#160;a.m. local time. The captain of the ship was reported to have died in the incident.<sup id="cite_ref-TRT_15_April_2022_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TRT_15_April_2022-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Russia has not announced any casualties in the fire or the sinking of the ship. American sources believed there were casualties in the event.<sup id="cite_ref-BBC_Moskva_crew_55-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBC_Moskva_crew-55">&#91;55&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
